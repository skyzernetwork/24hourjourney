# The hidden problem with using too many fonts

Typography plays a major role in how websites communicate personality and structure. But using too many fonts at once can quickly make a design feel inconsistent.

A common mistake is mixing several unrelated typefaces together without a clear system. This creates visual clutter and makes layouts harder to read.

Most modern websites stay within one or two font families and rely on weight, size, and spacing to create variation instead of constantly switching styles.

Good typography usually feels invisible. It supports the content without distracting from it.

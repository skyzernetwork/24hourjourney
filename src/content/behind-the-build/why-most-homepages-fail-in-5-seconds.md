# Why most homepages fail in 5 seconds

The average visitor does not carefully read a homepage from top to bottom. Most people scan quickly looking for answers to three things:
- What is this?
- Can I trust it?
- Where do I go next?

If those questions are unclear, users usually leave within seconds.

A lot of websites fail because they overload visitors with animations, large walls of text, or vague branding instead of simple communication. Clear headlines, spacing, and structure often matter more than flashy visuals.

Good homepages are less about showing everything and more about guiding attention toward the most important information.
